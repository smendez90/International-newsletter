Director’s Message

Fall semester was a great success! We had over 100 alumni join us in celebrating the 70th anniversary of the International House and a standing-room-only crowd at the UN Day Tea. Our students were thrilled to receive so much support from their predecessors, and we hope you enjoyed meeting and spending time with them as well.

We also had 15 students make the President’s and Dean’s Lists and an average GPA of 3.09 for the International House students. We’re very proud of all our students for working hard to achieve their academic goals while also representing their home countries and the International House at various events.

This spring, we’re looking forward to more opportunities to serve the community and spread cultural awareness, especially at the Girl Scout’s World Thinking Day in February and the Taster’s Fair, which will be April 8 and is open to the public. We hope you’ll follow us on Facebook and join us for any public events you’re able to attend. We hope to hear from you or see you at events in the future.

Thank you,

Chandni Khadka
Director, International House & Programs
Joe Serviss

Secretary/Treasurer of the International Endowment Foundation, Inc.

As a student at JSU in 1964, I had a few classes with international students, but I wasn’t really involved with the International House. After I retired from the army in 1989, I returned to JSU and in 1991, my son joined the International House as a freshman. As the vice president of institutional advancement, I was lucky enough to attend the 60th anniversary reunion in Paris, France and reacquaint myself with old friends and make new ones as well. In 2009, I retired from the university, and I was invited to serve on the International Endowment Foundation Board. I’ve had a long and varied history with the International House, and I hope to see it continue for at least another 70 years. It has been a unique program for JSU, the community, the state, and the country for a long time, and I hope its traditions and ideals will continue to have a positive effect on our campus and the world for a long time to come.

The alumni who have shaped and benefitted from this excellent program are important to the university and to the International Program, and I hope they have memories of us that are as fond as our memories of them. I hope they are inspired by their experiences to contribute to the continuation of the International House. Financial support for the program is more important today than it has been in the past. We’re going to need everyone to contribute so we can continue the program for future students. Please send donations through the online method or send checks payable to Jones International House Program c/o Jacksonville State University 700 Pelham Road North Jacksonville, AL 36265. To make your gift online please follow the link to our secure on-line giving page.

Thank you,
Joe Serviss
Secretary/Treasurer

Vasila Nuralieva

Tajikistan

How did you find out about the International House? I heard about the International House last year from my Polish host Dad. He used to live in the house back in 2000. This program is unknown in my home country, and it was the first time I heard about it. He is the one who encouraged me with his stories and his life at JSU. I am so lucky because who knew that I will meet a person far from my home country who will encourage me to apply to the House.

What is your favorite thing about the House? The House is different from other dormitories; this makes it special. The diversity makes it unique. Every resident is so different, with diverse backgrounds and cultures; it makes us special and it unites us. I would never think before that I would meet so many people from South America or Africa. That part of the world was unattainable for me. Now, I can say for sure that someday I will visit these countries, and I will be glad to have a reunion with all the House members. Moreover, I like the idea of sharing a room with an American. It helps me to adapt more easily as my roommate is always the one who gives me her hand. That’s what I appreciate most.

What would you like to say to people from the campus or community about the House? If someone wants to widen their outlook, the House is the best place. It shows all the beauty of the world. I have been in many diverse societies, but the House is more than a diverse society. The knowledge and experience that you get in the House is priceless. Most likely, I would repeat my favorite things about the house, which I have mentioned above.

What would you like to say to alumni of the House? First, I want to thank my host Dad, as he is the one who informed me about the program and encouraged me to apply. Afterward, I want to thank every single alumni of the House for their support, for keeping the House traditions.
Mason Battles

Hokes Bluff, Alabama

How did you find out about the International House? As a freshman, I walked across campus in front of the International House just as most students did. After the first month of classes, curiosity got the best of me and I walked in. I climbed up the stairs and asked “what is the International House?" I was directed to the house coordinator at that time, Mrs. Ena Aguilar. She explained to me that the International House was a place for a globalized cultural exchange, gave me a DVD about the history, and invited me to the U.N. Day Tea.

What is your favorite thing about the International House? My favorite thing about the International House is the people. This program brings a type of person that the university setting needs: open-minded, creative, and willing to learn. An old newspaper hangs framed on our basement wall. The article is an interview very similar to this one. When asked this question, the American student says "The people made me stay." I think my answer is pretty close to that too.

What would you like to say about the International House to JSU campus/community? I would like people to know that the International House isn't a secluded place. We do have times where we focus on growing together and moving forward, but that doesn't have to be an isolated thing. The International House is simply a host or home-base to the start of Jacksonville's global community.

What would you like to say to the alumni? To alumni I say thank you. Your contributions and support to the university and to the Jones International House Program are greatly appreciated. For a program to last for over 70 years is a huge accomplishment. Our past has been built on people who believed in this program. Our future is in the hands of those who still do. I only hope the future can be handled with delicate hands and that the International House can be a global beacon of opportunity in our very own gem of the hills.
